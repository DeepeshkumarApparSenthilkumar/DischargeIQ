## Template text

Your doctor took out your old hip joint and put in a new one. The new joint is made of metal and plastic. It needs time to set in place. For six weeks, follow these three rules. Do not cross your legs. Do not lean forward past your waist. Do not turn your foot inward. These rules keep the new joint from slipping out. Most people can walk with help the day after surgery. Light activity is okay after six weeks. Full healing takes three to six months.
